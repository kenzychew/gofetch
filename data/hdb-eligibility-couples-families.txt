HDB Flat Eligibility - Couples and Families
Source: hdb.gov.sg/buying-a-flat/flat-grant-and-loan-eligibility/couples-and-families
Last updated: April 2026

Understand the eligibility conditions, the types of flats, CPF housing grants and HDB housing loan that you may apply for, based on your household type.

Apply for an HDB Flat Eligibility (HFE) letter via the HDB Flat Portal for a holistic understanding of your housing and financing options before you embark on your home buying journey. It will inform you upfront of your eligibility to buy a new or resale flat, as well as the amount of CPF housing grants and HDB housing loan you are eligible for.

Core family nucleus: A household's eligibility for housing subsidies and HDB housing loan is assessed based on the core family nucleus, which is formed by the core member(s). Core members refer to the applicant(s) and occupier(s) in an HFE letter application who enable the household to qualify for a flat purchase under an eligibility scheme. All core members must remain in the flat application, and physically reside in the flat during the minimum occupation period (MOP) after the flat purchase.

Eligibility conditions for Couples and Families (Fiance and Fiancee):

Flat type:
- New Flats / Resale Plus / Resale Prime: Up to a 5-room flat
- Resale Unclassified / Standard Flats: All flat types (excluding 3Gen flats)

Note: Prime flats include Prime Location Public Housing (PLH) flats sold before October 2024 sales exercise. Unclassified resale flats refer to flats sold before October 2024 sales exercise and not classified as Standard, Plus or Prime.

Citizenship:
- New Flats / Resale Plus / Resale Prime: Must be a Singapore Citizen (SC) and include at least 1 other SC or Singapore Permanent Resident (SPR)
- Resale Unclassified / Standard: Must be an SC or SPR, include at least 1 occupant who is an SC or SPR. For SPR households: all applicants and core occupiers must have SPR status for at least 3 years.

Age: At least 21 years old

Monthly household income ceiling (total income of all persons listed in the HFE letter application):
- 2-room Flexi flat (99-year lease): $7,000
- 3-room flat: $14,000 or $7,000, depending on project (press release will specify)
- 3-room flat: $21,000 if buying with extended or multi-generation family in projects with $14,000 income ceiling
- 4- and 5-room flat: $14,000
- 4- and 5-room flat: $21,000 if buying with extended or multi-generation family
- Resale Unclassified / Standard Flats: No income ceiling
Note: Income ceiling applies to qualify for CPF housing grants (excluding Proximity Housing Grant) and HDB housing loan.

Ownership / interest in property:

You are considered to own or have an interest in a property if you and/or your spouse have acquired a property through purchase, gift, inheritance, nominees, or trust.

Private residential property rules:
All applicants and occupiers must not own or have an interest in any local or overseas private residential property.

Disposal waiting periods:
- Buying from HDB / resale Plus or Prime / resale with CPF grants / HDB loan: At least 30 months before HFE letter application from legal completion date of disposal
- Buying non-subsidised resale unclassified/Standard flat: At least 15 months before HFE letter application from legal completion date

Exception for seniors aged 55+: The waiting period does not apply to Singapore citizens aged 55+ buying short-lease 2-room Flexi flat or Community Care Apartment. They must dispose of private property within 6 months of flat purchase completion.

Non-residential property rules:
- Buying from HDB / resale Plus or Prime / with CPF grants / HDB loan: Can own up to 1 non-residential property. More than 1 requires disposal at least 30 months before HFE application.
- Buying non-subsidised resale unclassified/Standard: No restriction on number of non-residential properties.

Previous housing subsidies:
A subsidised housing unit refers to: a flat bought from HDB, resale flat with CPF grants, DBSS flat, EC unit from developer, or other forms (SERS, privatisation of HUDC).

Any previous housing subsidy does not affect eligibility to buy a resale flat. However, the number of subsidies taken affects eligibility for CPF housing grants.

Resale levy: If you and/or any core member(s) have taken a housing subsidy, you are considered a second-timer. All second-timer core member(s) will need to pay their respective resale levies when buying a flat from HDB.
