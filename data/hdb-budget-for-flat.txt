Budget for a Flat

The second step to the ABCs of financial planning is to work out your Budget based on available funds.

Before you embark on your home buying journey, apply for an HDB Flat Eligibility (HFE) letter to find out your eligibility to buy a new or resale flat as well as the amount of CPF housing grants and HDB housing loan you are eligible for.

Estimating your housing budget

You can work out an estimated housing budget by adding up the following:
- Available cash savings
- Available CPF Ordinary Account (OA) savings (each flat applicant may retain up to $20,000)
- Probable CPF housing grants
- Estimated housing loan from HDB or the participating financial institutions (FIs)

The amount you may borrow from HDB or FIs is generally determined by your age, income, and financial standing.

If you need a housing loan to buy a flat, please check your loan eligibility with HDB or approach the FIs regulated by the Monetary Authority of Singapore. View and compare housing loan packages offered by the participating FIs and understand the key terms and conditions before making an informed financing decision.

Ensure that you have obtained sufficient financing before proceeding to buy a flat.

Understand the payments required

A housing loan from HDB or FI can only be used towards the purchase price of the flat (up to the prevailing loan-to-value limits). The HDB housing loan amount will be pro-rated from the prevailing loan-to-value limits if the flat's lease does not cover the youngest applicant to the age of 95 and beyond.

For resale flats, the purchase price refers to the lower of the resale price or value of the flat.

Be sure to set aside money for the upfront payments required in cash or CPF savings for your intended flat, before committing to buy.

New Flat payments:
- Booking fee, downpayment and related costs at various stages of the flat purchase from HDB.

Resale Flat payments:
- Initial payment and related costs
- Lump sum payment in cash, where applicable:
  - Deposit to flat seller
  - Property agent's fees
  - Cash payment for the difference between resale price and flat's value

Use of CPF savings

You may use your available CPF Ordinary Account savings to pay the downpayment or initial payment. If your CPF savings are not enough, you will have to pay the balance amount in cash.

Use CPF Board's housing usage calculator to estimate the amount of CPF savings you can use for your flat purchase, and their home purchase planner to understand how the use of CPF savings for a flat purchase impacts your retirement payouts.

Other payments to consider:
- Renovation, furnishing and moving expenses
- Wedding expenses, if applicable

Financial tools available:
- Calculate housing budget: Work out an estimated housing budget with housing loan information provided by HDB and the participating FI.
- Create a payment plan: Work out a customised financial plan for your intended flat purchase. Understand the payments required at the various milestones of your flat buying journey and check if your budget is sufficient.
- Calculate sale proceeds: Work out the estimated cash proceeds you may receive from the sale of your current flat and how much you need to set aside for your next flat purchase.
